Process

How I work

A practical, repeatable process that turns ideas into usable products — informed by Google UX methodology and hands-on practice.

Step 01 Discover

Empathize & Research

Understand the user & the problem
  • Stakeholder & user interviews to uncover real needs and frustrations.
  • Competitor & pattern analysis — what already works, what doesn't.
  • Build personas, empathy maps, and journey maps as a shared source of truth.
Interviews Personas Empathy Maps Journey Maps
Step 02 Define

Frame the Problem

Turn research into clear direction
  • Synthesize findings into problem statements and design goals.
  • Map user flows and information architecture for the key journeys.
  • Prioritize features by impact, effort, and user value.
User Flows IA Problem Statements
Step 03 Ideate

Sketch & Wireframe

Explore solutions cheaply, before pixels
  • Quick sketches and low-fidelity wireframes to validate structure.
  • Iterate on layout, content priority, and navigation patterns.
  • Align with stakeholders before committing to visual design.
Sketches Lo-Fi Wireframes Figma
Step 04 Design

High-Fidelity UI

Make it beautiful, brand-aligned & consistent
  • Apply typography, color, spacing, and visual hierarchy with intention.
  • Build out reusable components and auto layout for a consistent system.
  • Design for accessibility — contrast, touch targets, and readability.
Hi-Fi Screens Components Auto Layout Design Tokens
Step 05 Prototype & Test

Prototype, Test & Refine

Validate with real users — iterate fast
  • Build interactive Figma prototypes that feel like the real product.
  • Run usability tests; observe what works and what confuses people.
  • Refine flows, micro-interactions, and copy based on what we learn.
Interactive Prototype Usability Testing Iteration
